<p><p>The oral emergency contraceptive pill market primarily consists of two types: progestin-only contraceptives and combined hormonal contraceptives. Progestin-only contraceptives, often referred to as the "mini-pill," contain only progesterone, providing a simpler mechanism for preventing ovulation and reducing the risk of fertilization. In contrast, combined hormonal contraceptives consist of both estrogen and progesterone, offering a broader mechanism of action. These options cater to varying consumer preferences and health needs, influencing accessibility and adoption in different demographics.</p></p>
